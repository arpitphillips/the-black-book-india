# Types of Photographers in India

Photography in India is a vast and dynamic field. The cultural landscape, massive wedding industry, and growing commercial sectors have given rise to various specialized niches. Below is an individual list of professional photographer types in India:

## 1. Wedding Photographer
Covers pre-wedding shoots, the main wedding ceremonies, and post-wedding functions. They often blend traditional, posed photography with modern, candid styles to capture multi-day Indian wedding rituals.

## 2. Portrait & Family Photographer
Focuses on capturing individuals, couples, or families. This includes specialized shoots like maternity, newborn, and milestone photography.

## 3. Lifestyle Photographer
Takes a candid approach aimed at telling a story or capturing natural, unposed, everyday moments of individuals or families.

## 4. Product & E-commerce Photographer
Captures items in detail to make them appealing for online marketplaces, catalogs, and print sales.

## 5. Food Photographer
A rapidly growing niche fueled by social media, food blogging, culinary apps, and the restaurant industry.

## 6. Fashion Photographer
Works closely with models, stylists, makeup artists, and designers to showcase clothing, accessories, and trends for magazines, brands, and advertising.

## 7. Real Estate & Architectural Photographer
Focuses on capturing buildings, interiors, and properties for real estate sales, hospitality businesses, or architectural design portfolios.

## 8. Wildlife Photographer
A challenging field requiring patience and specialized telephoto equipment to document animals in their natural habitats across India's many national parks and reserves.

## 9. Landscape Photographer
Captures natural scenes such as mountains (e.g., the Himalayas), forests, deserts, and coastal seascapes.

## 10. Travel Photographer
Blends landscapes, culture, portraiture, and street photography to transport the viewer to a specific location and convey the essence of a place.

## 11. Aerial/Drone Photographer
Utilizes drones to capture unique, elevated perspectives of landscapes, cities, real estate, or large-scale events.

## 12. Street Photographer
Documents candid, everyday life in public spaces, capturing the vibrant essence, chaos, and beauty of Indian cities and towns.

## 13. Documentary/Photojournalist
Portrays significant news events, social issues, or historical moments to tell a factual, impactful story for publications or media outlets.

## 14. Fine Art Photographer
Focuses on creative photography intended for display in galleries, exhibitions, or personal artistic expression. In this category, the photographer has complete freedom in composition, editing, and conceptual execution.

## 15. Event Photographer
Captures corporate events, concerts, cultural festivals, and other large gatherings, ensuring key moments and attendees are documented.
